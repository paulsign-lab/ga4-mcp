# Playbook: B2B Lead Site

This playbook applies when the site sells to businesses via quotes, samples,
contracts, or partnerships — not retail checkout. Typical signals: no
"Add to Cart" button on product pages, "Contact Us" or "Request a Quote" as
the primary CTA, $0.00 prices or "Contact for Pricing", industry-segmented
collections, downloadable catalogues/spec sheets.

## KPI definition — Qualified Lead

The single most important sentence in this playbook:

> **Qualified Lead = `request_quote` + `contact_form_submit` + `catalog_download_complete`**

These three events, summed, define one monthly KPI: total qualified leads.
Everything else (page views, scroll depth, video plays) is upstream funnel
context. Do not let the user fall in love with vanity metrics — bring every
conversation back to this single KPI.

Secondary KPI: **Lead Efficiency = Sessions / Qualified Lead** (lower is
better). B2B benchmark: 50-150 sessions per qualified lead.

## Funnel — 5 stages

| Stage | Event | B2B Benchmark | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Site visit | `page_view` | — | Self-explanatory |
| 2. Product interest | `view_item` | ~20% of sessions | PDP load |
| 3. Conversion intent | `view_item_list` + `contact_form_view` + `catalog_view` | ~4% of sessions | Reached a high-intent page |
| 4. Conversion start | `request_quote` clicked, `contact_form_start`, `catalog_download_start` | ~1.5% of sessions | Showed lead intent |
| 5. Qualified Lead | `request_quote` submitted + `contact_form_submit` + `catalog_download_complete` | ~0.6% of sessions | The KPI |

## Event matrix — 20 events

| # | event_name | Type | Conversion? | Trigger | Required params |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `page_view` | Standard | No | All pages | page_location, page_title, page_type |
| 2 | `session_start` | Standard | No | Session begin | (auto) |
| 3 | `scroll` | Enhanced | No | 90% scroll | (auto) |
| 4 | `click` (outbound) | Enhanced | No | External link | link_url, link_domain |
| 5 | `view_item_list` | Recommended | No | PLP load | item_list_name (=industry), items[] |
| 6 | `view_item` | Recommended | No | PDP load | items[] (item_id=product_handle, item_category=industry) |
| 7 | `select_item` | Recommended | No | PLP card click | items[] |
| 8 | `nav_click` | Custom | No | Header/footer nav click | nav_label, nav_section, nav_target |
| 9 | `request_quote` | Custom | **YES ★** | PDP CTA click ("Contact Us" / "Request a Quote") | product_handle, product_title, industry, source_section |
| 10 | `contact_form_view` | Custom | No | Contact page load | form_location, referrer_page |
| 11 | `contact_form_start` | Custom | No | Contact form first focus | form_location |
| 12 | `contact_form_submit` | Custom | **YES ★** | Contact form submit | form_location, industry, inquiry_type, company, country |
| 13 | `catalog_view` | Custom | No | Catalogue page load | page_location, referrer_page |
| 14 | `catalog_download_start` | Custom | No | Klaviyo/gating form submit | email_provided=true, industry |
| 15 | `catalog_download_complete` | Custom | **YES ★** | PDF download success | file_name, industry |
| 16 | `file_download` | Enhanced | YES | PDF/spec sheet click | file_name, file_extension, file_path |
| 17 | `phone_click` | Custom | YES | tel: click | phone_number, page_location |
| 18 | `email_click` | Custom | YES | mailto: click | email_address, page_location |
| 19 | `newsletter_subscribe` | Recommended | YES | Footer newsletter submit | form_location=footer, source_page |
| 20 | `klaviyo_form_submit` | Custom | YES | Klaviyo form (non-catalog) submit | klaviyo_form_id, source_page, industry |

★ = the three events that compose Qualified Lead.

Optional add-ons depending on site:
- `video_play` for branded video content
- `manual_view` for after-sales support analysis (Manuals page)

## Custom dimensions (Event-scope) — register 6 in GA4 Admin

| Dimension name | Parameter | Use |
|---|---|---|
| industry | `industry` | Split BABY/PETS/CARE/MEDICAL/etc. |
| inquiry_type | `inquiry_type` | quote / sample / support / partnership |
| product_handle | `product_handle` | Per-product analysis |
| product_category | `product_category` | Product series (Legacy/New Technology/etc.) |
| file_name | `file_name` | Which PDF was downloaded |
| form_location | `form_location` | header/footer/contact-page/pdp |

User Properties: only **`user_logged_in`** — B2B sites are anonymous-lead
heavy. Don't over-engineer User Properties unless the site has a dealer
portal with logged-in B2B accounts (add `customer_tier` then: retailer /
wholesaler / distributor).

## What goes into `gtm_container.json` for B2B

The base GTM container generated by `scripts/build_gtm.py` should contain:

- 1 × Google Tag (GA4 config) on All Pages
- 17 × GA4 Event tags, one per event #5-20 above (4 are auto-collected: 1-4)
- 3 × Meta Pixel tags: Base / Lead / CompleteRegistration
- 17 × Triggers matching the event matrix
- 15 × Variables (dataLayer variables + 1 lookup table + 2 constants + 1 JS macro)
- 4 × Folders: GA4 Tags, Triggers, Variables, Meta Pixel

The lookup table `lookup.pagePath_industry` maps URL paths to industries:
```
/collections/diaper-pails       → BABY
/collections/pet-waste-pails    → PETS
/collections/adult-care-disposal → CARE
...
```
This auto-attaches `industry` to every event without per-event code.

## Looker Studio dashboard — 1-page B2B lead view

Structure (6 rows × up to 4 widgets per row):

- Row 1 (KPI scorecards): yesterday's leads · this week · this month · sessions/lead ratio
- Row 2 (funnel/composition): 5-stage funnel · inquiry_type donut
- Row 3 (industry): leads by industry (bar) · PDP views by industry (bar)
- Row 4 (acquisition): top 10 channels (table) · top 10 landing pages (table)
- Row 5 (content): top 10 products by view_item (table) · top 5 downloaded assets
- Row 6 (trend): 30-day daily lead trendline · top 10 search queries (GSC)

The `industry` custom dimension is the differentiator. Without it, Row 3 is
impossible.

## SEO copywriting tone for B2B

When generating per-page Title / Meta Description for the SEO sheet of the
checklist, use these conventions:

- Include **"Wholesale" / "B2B" / "Trade"** in commercial pages (collection,
  contact, catalogue)
- Industry-segment collections: lead with the product keyword, follow with the
  industry. Example: "Diaper Pails Wholesale — Janibell B2B (AKORD · BiiNü ·
  Champ)"
- Brand Story page: emphasize provenance, decades, certifications, awards
- Sustainability page: cite specific certs (GRS, ISO 14001) and recycled
  material percentages
- Avoid retail words: "shop", "buy now", "sale". Use: "request", "quote",
  "partnership", "contact"

## Discovered-issues checklist (Step 5 of the workflow)

Issues to actively look for on a B2B site:

1. PDP has both "Add to Cart" AND no visible price → ambiguous CTA, recommend
   "Contact for Pricing"
2. Contact form missing B2B-critical fields (Company, Country, Industry,
   Inquiry Type, Quantity Range)
3. Catalog page exists but no Klaviyo gate or downloadable PDF
4. Industry-segmented collections use generic handles (`/collections/baby`)
   instead of keyword-rich handles → propose Pattern A migration (see
   `reference/url_handle_patterns.md`)
5. Old platform cookies still present (`_imweb_*`, `_cafe24_*`, `__bs_*`)
6. Vendor field set to a non-brand placeholder (e.g., `magikan.dev`)
7. Schema.org Organization missing
8. No Meta Pixel installed (recommend installing even if not running ads yet —
   builds remarketing pool)

## Common mistakes when applying this playbook

- **Don't add a `purchase` event for a B2B site.** Even if Shopify auto-emits
  one when checkout happens, exclude it from conversion tracking — the
  Qualified Lead trio (events 9/12/15) is the real conversion KPI.
- **Don't track `add_to_cart` on B2B.** Cart is rarely used (sites usually
  hide it). If it fires, it's noise.
- **Don't try to track decision-maker identity from anonymous traffic.** B2B
  identity comes from the post-form follow-up (CRM). GA4's role is to count
  and segment leads, not identify people.
- **Don't deploy 20 events on a site with <500 monthly visitors.** Start with
  events 1, 6, 9, 12, 15 (the absolute minimum for Qualified Lead) and add the
  rest as traffic grows.
